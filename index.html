Title of First Image

Hidden disabilities are conditions that are not immediately apparent or visible to others, such as mental health disorders, chronic pain, and learning disabilities. These conditions can be just as debilitating as physical disabilities, but individuals may face challenges in receiving recognition or accommodations due to the lack of visible symptoms. Hidden disabilities can impact a person's daily life, including their work, social interactions, and overall well-being. It is important for society to recognize and support individuals with hidden disabilities, as well as create inclusive environments that accommodate their needs. These disabilites can include Depression, Bipolar Disorder, Schizophrenia, PTSD, Dyslexia, along with many more. These disabilies are unseen by people walking on the street and can often times go without awareness and or help from others.

Recently there has been a greater effort on behalf of universities to admit and retain more disabled students. Among this population would be those with hidden disabilities. Although these students sometimes need support to learn in the best way the universities do not always give it. The main reason for this is the students with hidden disabilities need to reach out to the university for support. It is hard to admit you need help and it takes a lot of courage but the students need to understand that the unversity support staff are there to help.
